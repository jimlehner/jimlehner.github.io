<h1>The Broken Quality Initiative</h1>

<h2>Helping engineers unlock the power of SPC</h2>

<div class="bqi-layout">

  <!-- TEXT COLUMN -->
  <div class="bqi-text">
    <p>
      Most manufacturing efforts use conformance to specification as their guiding light. Rather than operate processes <b>on-target with minimum variance</b>—the definition of quality established by Dr. Genichi Taguchi in September 1960—quality, as defined in ISO 9000:2015 Clause 3.5.1, is defined as <em>the degree to which a set of inherent characteristics of an object fulfills requirements</em>.
    </p>
    <p>
      While on its face this definition seems sensible—after all no customer in their right mind will accept product that does not fulfill requirements—it reinforces the perspective that quality is binary. It bolsters the widely held but wrong belief that the measured characteristics of parts, products, services, processes, and systems are either good or bad, conforming or nonconforming.
    </p>

    <p>
      As a result of this binary view, the quality and manufacturing landscape has been infected with tools, techniques, methods, and fads that are too busy mopping to turn off the water. The field has been reduced to efforts that attempt to improve quality and reduce costs without any knowledge or recognition of variation and its influence. This results in actions that ignore the reality that variation is always at work. It ensures that defects will be produced and world-class quality products will remain out of reach.
    </p>

      At <a href="https://brokenquality.com" target="_blank">The Broken Quality Initiative</a> we are on a mission to correct manufacturing’s course. We are on a mission to teach every engineer the theory and practice that make world-class quality products possible.
