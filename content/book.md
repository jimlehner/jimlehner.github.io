<h1>The Virus of Variation</h1>
<h2>Making Sense of Death and Data using Process Behavior Charts</h2>

<div class="book-layout">

  <div class="book-body">
    <p>
      Using the story of the 19th-century Hungarian physician, Ignaz Semmelweis, as a guide, <em>The Virus of Variation</em> teaches readers how to make sense of data using <em>process behavior charts</em>. Also known as control charts, process behavior charts are the only tool capable of making the invisible world of variation visible, allowing us to anticipate the future based on the past. This knowledge helps individuals and teams stop being reactive and start being proactive. It replaces luck with a math- and science-based methodology that reduces costs, improves quality, and, at least for Semmelweis, saves the lives of people in need.
    </p>
    <p>Some of the topics covered include:</p>
    <ul>
        <li>How to buid a process behavior chart (control chart)</li>
        <li>How to characterize process behavior and take actions that facilitate improvement</li>
        <li>The right (and wrong) way to calculate process limits</li>
        <li>The principles of process improvement</li>
        <li>Much more!</li>
    </ul>
